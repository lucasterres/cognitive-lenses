# Scoring rubric

Each answer is scored 0–5 on four dimensions. Score what the answer **does for the asker**, not how sophisticated it looks.

## 1. Premise scrutiny

Did the answer examine the question's own assumptions before accepting them?

- **0–1** — Accepts the frame entirely; answers the question exactly as asked even where the framing is doubtful.
- **2–3** — Notes an assumption in passing but the recommendation ignores it.
- **4–5** — Identifies the load-bearing assumption, tests it, and the recommendation changes (or is explicitly conditioned) accordingly. *If the premise is genuinely sound, full marks for correctly not manufacturing doubt.*

## 2. Risk & blind-spot coverage

Are the material risks, second-order effects, and affected parties on the table?

- **0–1** — Happy path only.
- **2–3** — Mentions generic risks ("test thoroughly") without connecting them to this task's specifics.
- **4–5** — Names the specific failure modes that would actually hurt (who, when, how), including at least one non-obvious one. *For tasks with no material risk, full marks for not inventing any.*

## 3. Actionability

Can the asker act on this tomorrow morning?

- **0–1** — Abstract advice, no concrete steps.
- **2–3** — Steps exist but are unordered, or the first step is disproportionate.
- **4–5** — Concrete, correctly sequenced, with the cheapest informative step first.

## 4. Calibration

Is confidence proportionate to the evidence in the answer?

- **0–1** — Unhedged certainty (or paralysis) regardless of evidence.
- **2–3** — Generic hedging ("it depends") that doesn't say on *what*.
- **4–5** — States what is known, what is assumed, and what single piece of missing information would most change the answer.

## Cost

Recorded separately as visible-output word count. Cost is not a rubric dimension — it is the denominator the charts divide by.
